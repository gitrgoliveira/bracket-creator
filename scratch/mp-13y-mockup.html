TV display (TvDisplay) — fullscreen per-court scoreboard

The in-venue scoreboard: white background, large readable text, full team match in progress with all 5 individual matches and their scores as they accumulate. Adding the per-match lineup here means each row gets the right names instead of fallback round names.

Daihyosen (DH) row appears only when the team match is tied and it's a knockout match (not a pool). Pool ties stand; only elimination matches force a rep bout. The DH row's competitors are chosen freely from each team's roster — not necessarily the same players who fought in the 5 standard matches above.

Upcoming matches: for team matches the board shows only the single next team match — a team match is long (≈5 bouts) so queueing 2–3 ahead adds no value. The "next 2–3 upcoming" rule from §Roles at Each Shiaijo applies to individual matches, which are short and turn over fast.

Streaming overlay (StreamingOverlay) — OBS / vMix lower-third

Transparent-background lower-third the broadcaster keys onto their video feed. QR in the centre ("scan for results") is the call-to-action to the per-match results page. Team names flank the QR; the current bout's two competitors flank the bout score (kept — the team IV score is dropped, it's meaningless mid-match on a lower-third). Bottom-tier names come from the per-match lineup; whatever bout is current shows here, including a daihyosen.

Recommendation (scope decision for the bead)

Three distinct surfaces, three distinct decisions:

  • Viewer match-details — ship in this bead. Read-only table, plenty of room, lowest risk.
  • TV display (TvDisplay) — likely ship in this bead too; the per-bout name+score grid is a small addition on top of the existing court tile. Real risk is layout on smaller TVs.
  • Streaming overlay (StreamingOverlay) — split into a follow-up bead. The "current bout" surface needs its own product call (do we show the position kanji? team score? both?) and broadcasters will want a layout mock. Don't bundle it.

Net: this bead = viewer + TvDisplay. New bead = StreamingOverlay lineup rendering. That keeps the PR scoped to one product question per surface and avoids dragging a broadcaster review into a UAT pass focused on the in-venue audience.
